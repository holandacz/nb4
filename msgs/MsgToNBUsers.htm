SO WHO IS BEHIND NOBLOOD?

With so much questionable information on the web, it is only appropriate to wonder who is behind any site you visit. As Executive Director and Webmaster, allow me to share a bit of my professional background.

In 1982 I founded a company, now known as Advisor Media. Its first product was a PC database contact manager written in dBase II.  Soon it became apparent that there was a pressing need for information related to database application development. So I figured, why not start a magazine dedicated to covering this topic? I named it Data Based Advisor Magazine. Although most startup magazines quickly fail, the Data Based Advisor became the number one trade-journal in its niche. Then in 1995 when the magazine had reached a monthly circulation of about 50,000, I sold my interest in order to pursue personal goals.

In 1995 the internet was still in its infancy. Having a passion for the subject of what was then called bloodless healthcare, I saw an opportunity to develop a website that would facilitate collaboration among healthcare professionals and the public who were interested or involved in bloodless healthcare. So in 1996 I registered the domain noblood.com and began hiring staff as well as inviting volunteers. An invaluable help at this point was Jan Wade who generously guided NoBlood’s editorial direction.

You should have seen my home in San Diego!  It went from a bachelor-pad to a web-development hub. I must admit that I had mixed feelings when I removed the pool-table from the living room and replaced it with six workstation cubicles! The den became my office with a rack of servers on one wall connected to a dedicated T1 line. In total, there were 12 workstations throughout the house with laptops for the bathrooms (just kidding). It was exciting to collaborate with experts from around the world as we cataloged information and created feature articles. A high point was when Doctor Estioko, M.D., a cardiothoracic surgeon and medical director of the Transfusion-Free Medicine & Surgery Center at Saint John’s Health Center, invited me to watch him perform a quadruple by-pass operation without blood transfusion.  Though I have never liked operating-room scenes, I forced myself to stand on a stool for four hours as I watched the operation from start to finish. I must say that I was pretty pleased with myself for toughing it out.

HOW IS NOBLOOD.ORG FUNDED?

Although I was hoping to keeps things simple, it became necessary to incorporate. A California non-profit corporation was formed which we named Bloodless Healthcare International, Inc. It is a 501(c)3 federally tax-exempt charitable corporation. The operative word here is non-profit. While operating a non-profit organization is different than operating a for-profit corporation, they are the same in terms of the need to pay for operating expenses.

Being well… a little flush at the time from the sale of my interest in my software and magazine publishing company, I began pouring funds and energy into NoBlood. Aside from various attempts to invite donations, our mission has always been to focus on making the very best website possible.  The necessary financial help would come later.

Well, the time has come. I need to bring to your attention that I no longer have the personal resources necessary to carry the load of building and running NoBlood.org. Users of NoBlood frequently rave about how useful the site is. With that fact firmly in mind I am writing to let you know that we find ourselves at a very important juncture in our development.

For NoBlood to meeting the current and anticipated demands of time and resources, we need to establish a working operating budget. To meet this need, we have launched three programs to accommodate corporate and individual sponsoring. These are called Pixels Pages, Premium Hospital Listings and Premium Users.

PIXELS PAGES

NoBlood Pixels Pages are pages where blocks of pixels are purchased that contain images such as corporate logos. Pixels can be purchased in blocks of 100 pixels at $0.20 per pixel per year. This concept was inspired by the wildly successful website (www.milliondollarhomepage.com) where a college student sold a million pixels at a dollar a pixel. We have three different pixels pages, Healthcare, Corporate, People and a page for testing. Please note the following features:

  1. Mouse over images. When moving your mouse over the images/logos, image and information is presented.
  2. Link to website. When a person clicks on the image, they are taken to the company’s website.
  3. Click tracking. The number of times the image is clicked is presented in the popup.
  4. Pixels listing. Visitors can view a complete list of images and their associated companies.
  5. Top 10 pixels. Visitors can view the top 10 recent and overall clicked images.
  6. Search. Visitors can quickly enter search terms to find company pixels.

The Healthcare Pixels page is limited to companies and organizations providing transfusion alternatives and blood management related products and services.  By visiting this page you will notice that the initial response to this funding program has been very gratifying. If you or your company would like to present your product or service on the page, feel free to click on the grid and select the blocks you wish to use and/or reply to this message along with your contact information. The Healthcare Pixels page is shaping up to be the Who’s Who in transfusion alternatives and blood management. We encourage you to add a link to this page on any web-site and blog you may participate in.

The Corporate Pixels page is where non-healthcare related companies can purchase blocks of pixels to show their support for advancing this important mission.

The People Pixels page is where individuals can purchase blocks of pixels to show their support for advancing this mission. Only difference here is that images are limited to simple icons or face shots and there are no links to personal websites.

Each image may occupy up to 64 blocks of pixels. We would be happy to assist with preparing images for inclusion from available artwork.

Understandably, we reserve the right to reject any image deemed objectionable.

Once companies and hospitals have been given sufficient time to place their logo’s on the appropriate page, we will initiate a press campaign designed to draw visitors to see who the leaders are in blood management and transfusion alternatives. Will you be found there?
