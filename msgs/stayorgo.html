I must bring to your attention a matter very dear to my heart.

Where should we take this website during the next year and beyond? Can we continue?

Should we stay... or is it time for us to go?

Why the question?

It is important that you understand how NoBlood has been funded and developed to date and what is needed in order to go forward. In 1996, seeing an opportunity to create an online clearinghouse of information related to bloodless medicine and surgery, I founded noblood.org. Since then I have devoted substantial personal funds and many thousands of hours of time without compensation toward the development and operation of this website. I can no longer afford to do this without more help.

The good news: noblood.org continues to grow, as evidenced by the number of daily users and forum posts.
The bad news: The financial demands of keeping the website properly up-to-date will require a substantial increase in subscriptions and/or personal donations.

Who benefits from noblood.org?

At present noblood.org has more than 14,000 registered users. There are some 5,000 discussions along with popular articles on every possible topic related to transfusion alternatives. Noblood.org continues to rank number one in Internet searches related to this subject. Every month, tens of thousands of unique visitors come to noblood.org!

Should noblood.org stay?

Do you appreciate having:

  • A professional, high-quality, well maintained website devoted to transfusion alternatives?
  • An online directory of products, equipment and techniques related to transfusion alternatives?
  • A reputable place to learn from medical professionals, as well as those with firsthand experience in dealing with conditions and techniques involving healthcare without the use of allogeneic blood transfusions?
  • 24/7 online access to these resources?

If you do, then answer YES to the poll* at the top of this page, noblood.org should stay! Are you willing to contribute — even a small amount — to help noblood.org to continue improving?

Should noblood.org go?

Do you believe that:

  • Sufficient information regarding transfusion alternatives is readily available elsewhere.
  • There is no need to maintain a knowledge base of expertise and experience on this subject.
  • There is no need to maintain a directory of hospitals offering bloodless surgery.
  • There is no need for a centralized online community for healthcare professions to network and share life-saving information.
  • There is no need for a place to exchange information with healthcare professionals and members of the public who have expertise and interest in this subject.

If you agree with the above statements, then answer NO to the poll* at the top of this page, noblood.org should go.
