Pediatric Court Orders

Joseph Malak, MD - Pediatrician
Vassar Brothers Medical Center

 It is just after 11 PM on a Saturday and the phone rings. You are contacted by the anxious parents of a child who has been transferred to the Neonatal Intensive Care Unit of the regional medical center. They inform you that the Department of Social Services is seeking an emergency court order to transfuse. The trial will be held in three hours. On a practical level, how can you help this family? Can stipulations be made in the order that would encourage bloodless management? What has worked in some instances? Read more...

Topic Tags Project
One of our site development projects involves the tagging of articles and discussions as they relate to key bloodless healthcare conditions and treatments. For example, when you click on Anemia in the Conditions List on the front page, a list of over 130 articles and discussions is found. How accurate and complete is this list? NoBlood contains thousands of articles and discussions. One should be able to click on any of these topic 'tags' and immediately go to all the relevant articles and discussions regarding that subject. However, at the moment, it is a bit "hit and miss" and needs some intelligent filtering to make sure only relevant articles are included. At the moment we have one volunteer working at this, but we could do with a few more helping hands. Please visit this Project Task to learn more about how you can perhaps ‘adopt’ a topic tag of interest to you or Contact Us.
